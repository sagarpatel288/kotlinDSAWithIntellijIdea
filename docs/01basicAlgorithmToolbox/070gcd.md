## Definition

* The greatest common divisor GCD(a,b) of two positive integers a and b is the largest integer d that divides both a and b, without leaving any remainder. 
* The solution of the Greatest Common Divisor Problem was first described (but not discovered!) by the Greek mathematician Euclid, about 23 centuries ago. 
* But the name of a mathematician who discovered this algorithm, a century before Euclid described it, remains unknown.

## Formula

* Euclid's theory says that gcd(a, b) = a if b = 0.
* And it also says gcd(a, b) = gcd(b, a % b), if b > 0.
* So, we keep doing gcd(b, a % b) until the second term becomes 0.
* And then we keep going back to receive the answer of gcd(a, b).
* So, it is a recursive programming, or we might use backtracking.

## Application

* Suppose that you have 48 chocolates, and your friend has 18 chocolates.
* Now, we don't want to mix them. 
* But we want to put maximum possible, but equal numbers of chocolates from both of you into the boxes. 
* So that each box will have maximum possible chocolates, and each box will have the same number of chocolates.
* How many boxes will we need? How many chocolates will we put into each box? How do we calculate this?
* gcd(48, 18) = 6.
* `6` is the maximum possible chocolates each box can get equally.
* It means, we will need, 48/6 = 8 boxes for you, and 18/6 = 3 boxes for your friend.
